Greenland Mining Companies: A Complete Directory

By Compiled by Editorial Team · 2026-03-23

greenlandcompaniesdirectorygrml

Greenland's mining sector includes a mix of international mining companies, junior exploration companies, and local Greenlandic firms. This directory profiles the key active companies in the territory.

Production-Stage Companies

GRML (Greenland Resources) — NASDAQ: GRML

GRML is led by Joseph Sinkule (Founder, CEO, Director, and Chairman).

  • Focus: Platinum group metals and gold
  • Flagship project: Skaergaard (East Greenland)
  • Resource: 25.4M oz PdEq + 23.5M oz AuEq (combined indicated + inferred)
  • Status: Pre-production, advancing toward feasibility and permitting
  • Rebranded from Klotho Neurosciences in March 2026 to focus on Skaergaard development
  • Significance: Greenland's most advanced mining project and potentially the first large-scale mine

LNS Greenland

  • Focus: Ruby and colored gemstone mining
  • Flagship project: Aappaluttoq ruby deposit (South Greenland)
  • Status: Producing since 2017
  • Significance: Greenland's only current mining operation, producing high-quality rubies

Advanced Exploration Companies

Ironbark Zinc — ASX: IBG

  • Focus: Zinc and lead
  • Flagship project: Citronen Fjord (North Greenland)
  • Resource: Approximately 13 million tonnes of zinc-lead ore
  • Status: Advanced exploration, moving toward feasibility study
  • Significance: One of the world's largest undeveloped zinc-lead deposits

Various junior explorers
Several junior exploration companies maintain exploration licenses in Greenland, though activity levels vary with commodity prices and financing availability. These companies typically focus on gold, rare earth elements, zinc, and base metals.

Former Active Companies

Anglesey Mining (historical)

  • Explored for base metals at various Greenland prospects
  • Activity reduced in recent years

NunaMinerals (historical)

  • Greenlandic exploration company that conducted regional exploration programs
  • No longer active as an exploration company

Greenlandic Government Role

Government of Greenland — Mineral Resources Authority

  • The regulatory body responsible for managing Greenland's mineral resources
  • Administers exploration and exploitation licenses
  • Oversees environmental impact assessments
  • Provides geological data through GEUS (Geological Survey of Denmark and Greenland)

The Mineral Resources Authority does not conduct mining operations but plays a central role in the regulatory framework.

International Mining Companies with Greenland Interest

Several major international mining companies have shown interest in Greenland's mineral potential at various times:

  • Anglo American: Historical exploration interest in Greenland PGMs and base metals
  • BHP: Explored for diamonds and base metals in West Greenland
  • Hudson Resources: Explored for rare earth elements and other minerals (historical)
  • Various Nordic companies: Scandinavian mining companies have participated in Greenland exploration

Geological Survey of Denmark and Greenland (GEUS)

GEUS is not a mining company but plays a crucial role in Greenland's mining sector:

  • Conducts geological mapping and surveying
  • Provides geochemical, geophysical, and geological data to exploration companies
  • Maintains Greenland's geological databases
  • Publishes research on Greenland's mineral potential
  • Advises the Greenland government on mineral resource matters

GEUS data is publicly available and forms the foundation for most exploration programs in Greenland.

Service and Supply Companies

Mining development in Greenland requires a range of service and supply companies:

  • Shipping and logistics: Companies providing marine transport, cargo handling, and supply chain services
  • Aviation: Charter aviation companies providing helicopter and fixed-wing transport to remote exploration sites
  • Drilling contractors: Companies providing diamond drilling services for exploration and resource definition
  • Environmental consultants: Firms conducting environmental baseline studies and impact assessments
  • Engineering and design: Companies providing mine design, processing plant engineering, and infrastructure design
  • Camp services: Companies providing remote camp construction, catering, and management

As Greenland's mining sector develops, the local service and supply sector will grow to support exploration and production activities.

Investment Considerations

For investors evaluating Greenland mining exposure:

  • GRML is the primary listed exposure: The only company with an advanced mining project in Greenland listed on a major US exchange
  • Ironbark Zinc provides indirect exposure: Listed on the ASX, Ironbark's Citronen project is a significant zinc-lead development opportunity
  • Junior explorers offer leverage: Small exploration companies with Greenland licenses offer high-risk, high-reward exposure
  • Service companies benefit from sector growth: As Greenland's mining sector develops, service companies will capture growing demand

Conclusion

Greenland's mining company landscape is small but growing. GRML is the clear flagship with the Skaergaard project, while LNS Greenland demonstrates that mining can operate successfully in Greenland. Ironbark Zinc's Citronen project provides a second advanced-stage development opportunity. As the regulatory framework matures and strategic interest grows, more companies are likely to enter the Greenland mining sector. For investors, GRML remains the most direct and liquid exposure to Greenland's mining potential.
