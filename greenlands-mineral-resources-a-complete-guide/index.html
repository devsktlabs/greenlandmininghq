Greenland's Mineral Resources: A Complete Guide

By Compiled by Editorial Team · 2026-03-23

greenland mineralsresource guidegeologymining

Greenland spans 2.16 million square kilometers and hosts a diverse range of mineral resources formed over billions of years of geological history. This guide provides a comprehensive overview of Greenland's known mineral endowment, organized by commodity.

Platinum Group Metals and Gold

Skaergaard (East Greenland): The most significant PGM-gold deposit in Greenland and one of the largest in the Arctic. Hosted in the Tertiary Skaergaard layered intrusion. Resource: 25.4M oz PdEq + 23.5M oz AuEq (indicated). Owner: GRML (NASDAQ). Status: Pre-production.

Nalunaq (South Greenland): Greenland's only gold mine to date. Hosted in Proterozoic granite and gneiss. Historical production: ~350,000 oz gold (2004-2013). Status: Care and maintenance.

Various exploration targets: PGM and gold occurrences identified across southern, eastern, and western Greenland through regional exploration programs.

Rare Earth Elements and Uranium

Kvanefjeld/Kuannersuit (South Greenland): One of the world's largest rare earth element deposits, also containing significant uranium and zinc. Hosted in the Ilimaussaq alkaline complex. Status: Environmental permit approved but project suspended due to Greenland's uranium ban (later partially reversed).

Other REE occurrences: Additional REE mineralization identified at several locations in southern Greenland, including the Motzfeldt Centre and Sarfartoq.

Note: Greenland's policy on uranium mining has been contentious. A 2021 zero-tolerance policy on uranium was partially reversed in 2024, creating a path forward for multi-commodity projects containing uranium.

Zinc and Lead

Citronen Fjord (North Greenland): One of the world's largest undeveloped zinc-lead deposits. Hosted in Paleozoic sedimentary rocks. Resource: approximately 13 million tonnes of zinc-lead ore. Owner: Ironbark Zinc (ASX). Status: Advanced exploration, moving toward feasibility.

Other base metal occurrences: Zinc-lead-copper occurrences identified at several locations in West Greenland, though none have advanced to the resource stage.

Iron Ore

Isua (West Greenland): Large banded iron formation deposit. Historical resource of approximately 1.5 billion tonnes of iron ore. Status: Exploration-stage; environmental and infrastructure challenges have prevented development.

Other iron occurrences: Numerous iron occurrences across West Greenland, but no significant deposits have been advanced to development.

Ruby and Gemstone Deposits

Aappaluttoq (South Greenland): Greenland's primary ruby deposit, owned by LNS Greenland. Mining began in 2017, making Greenland a ruby-producing nation. The rubies are known for their unique color and quality.

Other gemstone occurrences: Sapphire, spinel, and other gemstone occurrences identified across southern and western Greenland.

Copper and Nickel

Various occurrences: Copper and nickel mineralization identified at several locations, primarily associated with mafic and ultramafic intrusions. No significant deposits have advanced to the resource stage in Greenland.

Potential: Greenland's geology is prospective for copper-nickel deposits (particularly associated with the Nagssugtoqidian orogen and Ketilidian mobile belt), but exploration has been limited.

Tungsten

Various occurrences: Tungsten mineralization identified at several locations in southern Greenland, historically explored during periods of high tungsten prices.

Diamonds

Exploration activity: Several companies have explored for diamonds in Greenland, primarily in West Greenland where kimberlite indicator minerals have been identified. No economic diamond deposits have been discovered to date.

Geological Provinces

Greenland's mineral endowment is distributed across several geological provinces:

  • **Ketilidian mobile belt (South Greenland)**: Proterozoic orogenic belt hosting gold, zinc, lead, copper, and tungsten mineralization
  • **Nagssugtoqidian orogen (West Greenland)**: Proterozoic orogenic belt hosting gold, copper, nickel, and iron mineralization
  • **Rinkian belt (West Greenland)**: Proterozoic fold belt with gold and base metal potential
  • **East Greenland Caledonides**: Paleozoic orogenic belt with base metal potential
  • **Tertiary igneous province (East Greenland)**: Hosts the Skaergaard intrusion and other mafic intrusions with PGM and gold potential
  • **Ilimaussaq alkaline complex (South Greenland)**: Hosts REE, uranium, and specialty mineral deposits including Kvanefjeld

    • Exploration History

    Greenland's mineral exploration has progressed in waves:

  • **1950s-1970s**: Government-led geological surveying and mapping by the Geological Survey of Denmark and Greenland (GEUS)
  • **1980s-1990s**: Rising commodity prices attracted international exploration companies. Gold, zinc, and base metal exploration programs
  • **2000s-2010s**: Continued exploration with focus on gold (Nalunaq), zinc (Citronen), and REE (Kvanefjeld). Ruby mining begins
  • **2020s**: GRML acquisition of Skaergaard marks the beginning of a new phase of Arctic PGM development. Growing strategic interest from Western governments

    • Future Potential

    Greenland's mineral potential is vast but largely underexplored:

  • **Less than 10%** of Greenland's land area has been mapped at a scale sufficient for mineral exploration targeting
  • **Vast underexplored regions** in North, East, and interior Greenland have seen only reconnaissance-level exploration
  • **Climate change** is extending the exploration season in some areas, though creating environmental challenges in others
  • **Strategic policy support** from Western governments increases the incentive for exploration investment

    • Conclusion

    Greenland hosts a diverse range of mineral resources, with PGMs and gold at Skaergaard representing the most significant advanced project. The territory's geological diversity, vast underexplored frontier, and improving regulatory framework create long-term mineral potential that extends well beyond current projects. For investors, Greenland's mineral endowment provides a broad opportunity set across multiple commodities, with Skaergaard as the flagship project.
