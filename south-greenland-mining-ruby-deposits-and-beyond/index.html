South Greenland Mining: Ruby Deposits and Beyond

By Compiled by Editorial Team · 2026-03-23

south greenlandrubyminingnalunaq

South Greenland is Greenland's most explored and mined region, with a history stretching back to the cryolite mines of the 19th century. Today, South Greenland hosts active ruby mining, a care-and-maintenance gold mine, and one of the world's largest undeveloped rare earth deposits.

The Aappaluttoq Ruby Mine

South Greenland's only active mining operation:

  • Owner: LNS Greenland
  • Location: Near Qeqertarsuatsiaat, approximately 200 km south of Nuuk
  • Commodity: Ruby and pink sapphire
  • Status: Producing since 2017
  • Significance: Greenland's first colored gemstone mine, producing rubies known for their unique color and quality
  • Operations: Open-pit mining with alluvial processing. Small-scale by mining industry standards but significant for Greenland
  • Markets: Rough gemstones sold to international gem cutters and jewelers

The Aappaluttoq mine demonstrates that commercial mining can operate successfully in Greenland, providing a precedent for larger projects like Skaergaard.

Nalunaq Gold Mine (Care and Maintenance)

South Greenland's gold mining history:

  • Location: Near Nanortalik in southernmost Greenland
  • Historical production: Approximately 350,000 ounces of gold (2004-2013)
  • Mining method: Started as open-pit, transitioning to underground
  • Closure reason: Low gold prices and operational challenges
  • Current status: On care and maintenance. Several companies have expressed interest in restarting operations
  • Potential: If gold prices remain elevated and financing is available, Nalunaq could restart, providing additional gold production from Greenland

Kvanefjeld Rare Earth Deposit

One of the world's largest undeveloped REE deposits:

  • Location: Near the town of Narsaq in South Greenland
  • Owner: Greenland Minerals (historically)
  • Commodity: Rare earth elements, uranium, zinc
  • Scale: One of the world's largest REE resources
  • Status: Project suspended following Greenland's uranium policy changes. Environmental permit had been approved but project did not proceed
  • Future: The deposit remains one of the world's most significant undeveloped REE sources. Changes in uranium policy could potentially allow the project to proceed

The Kvanefjeld deposit is strategically significant for the rare earth supply chain, particularly for heavy rare earths used in permanent magnets and defense applications.

Other South Greenland Prospects

Gold exploration:

  • Multiple exploration companies have identified gold occurrences across South Greenland
  • The Ketilidian mobile belt is prospective for orogenic gold deposits
  • Exploration has been intermittent, dependent on commodity prices and financing

Zinc-lead:

  • Historical zinc-lead occurrences in the Ketilidian belt
  • No significant deposits have advanced to development

Tungsten:

  • Historical tungsten exploration and small-scale mining in South Greenland
  • Interest in tungsten resurfaces during periods of high tungsten prices

Graphite and other minerals:

  • Various occurrences of graphite, niobium, tantalum, and other minerals have been identified
  • No significant deposits have advanced to development

South Greenland Communities

South Greenland is the most populated region outside of Nuuk:

  • Qaqortoq: Approximately 3,200 people. Regional center for South Greenland
  • Narsaq: Approximately 1,400 people. Near Kvanefjeld. Community divided on mining development
  • Nanortalik: Approximately 1,200 people. Near Nalunaq
  • Qeqertarsuatsiaat: Small settlement near Aappaluttoq

These communities have the most experience with mining in Greenland and have developed both support for and concerns about mining development.

Infrastructure in South Greenland

South Greenland has the best-developed infrastructure outside of Nuuk:

  • Airports: Narsarsuaq airport provides international and domestic flights
  • Ports: Several towns have port facilities capable of handling small cargo vessels
  • Roads: Limited road networks around larger towns (no inter-town roads)
  • Power: Primarily diesel-generated, with some hydropower

Future Outlook

South Greenland's mining future depends on several factors:

  • Gold price: Sustained high gold prices could support Nalunaq restart and new gold exploration
  • Rare earth demand: Growing demand for REEs in clean energy technologies could revive Kvanefjeld or other REE projects
  • Ruby market: LNS Greenland's ruby mine provides a model for small-scale, high-value mineral production
  • Community support: Mining development requires ongoing community engagement and benefit sharing

Conclusion

South Greenland is Greenland's most established mining region, with a history of successful mining operations (cryolite, gold, rubies) and significant undeveloped deposits (Kvanefjeld REE). The Aappaluttoq ruby mine demonstrates that modern mining can operate successfully in Greenland. For investors, South Greenland provides a more explored and understood mining environment than East Greenland, with established communities, infrastructure, and precedent operations. However, the Kvanefjeld uranium controversy demonstrates that political and community factors remain significant considerations for Greenland mining development.

> Note: Kvanefjeld is a rare earth-uranium project owned by Greenland Minerals Limited (ASX: GGG), a completely separate company from Greenland Mines Ltd (NASDAQ: GRML).
