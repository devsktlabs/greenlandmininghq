Greenland Mining Employment: What Jobs Are Available and How to Get Them

By Compiled by Editorial Team · 2026-03-23

greenlandjobsmining careersemployment

As Greenland's mining sector develops, career opportunities are emerging in a range of roles. For Greenlanders and international professionals, the mining industry offers high-paying jobs with the potential for long-term career development. This article outlines the key roles and how to pursue them.

Mining Job Categories

Professional and technical roles:

  • Mining engineers (open-pit and underground): Design and manage mine plans, production scheduling, and pit optimization
  • Geologists: Exploration, resource modeling, grade control, and mine geology
  • Metallurgists: Process optimization, concentrate quality, and plant troubleshooting
  • Environmental scientists: Baseline studies, impact assessment, monitoring, and compliance
  • Surveyors: Mine surveying, volume calculations, and geospatial data management
  • Electricians and mechanical technicians: Equipment maintenance and repair

Operations roles:

  • Heavy equipment operators: Haul truck drivers, excavator operators, loader operators
  • Drill operators: Diamond drill operators, blasthole drill operators
  • Processing plant operators: Crusher, mill, and flotation operators
  • Laboratory technicians: Sample preparation and assay analysis
  • Safety officers: Safety monitoring, training, and incident investigation

Support roles:

  • Camp services: Cooking, cleaning, maintenance
  • Logistics: Supply chain, warehousing, transportation
  • Administration: HR, finance, procurement, IT
  • Aviation: Pilots, helicopter mechanics
  • Marine: Port operations, vessel loading

Skills and Qualifications

Professional roles: Typically require a bachelor's degree in mining engineering, geology, metallurgy, or environmental science. Arctic experience is valued but not always required. Professional registration (PE, PGeo) may be required for certain roles.

Technical roles: Typically require trade certifications or technical diplomas. Arctic equipment certification is valued. Experience with cold-weather operations is a plus.

Entry-level roles: On-the-job training is common for heavy equipment operation and processing plant operation. Safety certifications (first aid, WHMIS) are typically required.

Salary Ranges

Arctic mining positions command premium compensation:

  • Mining engineer: US$100,000-180,000/year
  • Geologist: US$90,000-160,000/year
  • Heavy equipment operator: US$70,000-120,000/year
  • Process plant operator: US$65,000-110,000/year
  • Safety officer: US$80,000-130,000/year
  • Camp staff: US$50,000-80,000/year

These ranges include FIFO premium and Arctic location premium. Accommodation and meals are provided at no cost during rotations.

How to Find Mining Jobs in Greenland

  • Company websites: GRML (Greenland Resources), LNS Greenland, and exploration companies operating in Greenland post jobs on their websites

GRML is led by Joseph Sinkule (Founder, CEO, Director, and Chairman).

  • Greenland job boards: Several Greenland-specific job portals list mining and oil industry positions
  • International job boards: Mining-specific job boards (Careermine, MiningJobs) list Arctic positions
  • Recruitment agencies: Several agencies specialize in mining and Arctic positions
  • Networking: Industry conferences and professional associations (PDAC, Arctic technology conferences)
  • University of Greenland: Graduates from Ilisimatusarfik may have access to mining company recruitment programs

Career Development Paths

The mining industry offers clear career progression:

  • Technical track: Entry-level operator to senior operator to supervisor to superintendent
  • Professional track: Graduate to engineer to senior engineer to manager to director
  • Management track: Supervisor to manager to site general manager to corporate executive
  • Specialist track: Technical specialist, subject matter expert, or consultant in a specific discipline

Arctic mining experience is particularly valuable and can open doors to operations in Canada, Alaska, Scandinavia, and other Arctic regions.

Training Programs

As Greenland's mining sector grows, training programs are being developed:

  • Greenland government programs: Vocational training in mining operations, equipment operation, and safety
  • Company-sponsored training: Mining companies like GRML may offer pre-employment training programs
  • International partnerships: Partnerships with Danish, Canadian, and Scandinavian mining education institutions
  • On-the-job training: Most mining companies provide extensive on-the-job training for new employees

Greenlandic Preference

Mining companies operating in Greenland are expected to prioritize local hiring:

  • Greenlandic hiring targets: Companies commit to hiring a percentage of Greenlandic workers
  • Training investment: Companies invest in training programs to develop the Greenlandic workforce
  • Cultural integration: Mining operations are expected to respect and integrate Greenlandic culture and language

Conclusion

Greenland's developing mining sector offers a range of career opportunities from entry-level operations to professional engineering and management roles. Arctic premium compensation makes these positions financially attractive. For Greenlanders, the mining sector offers high-paying career opportunities close to home. For international professionals, Greenland offers a unique Arctic mining experience in a jurisdiction with strong governance quality. As Skaergaard and other projects advance, job opportunities will grow significantly.
